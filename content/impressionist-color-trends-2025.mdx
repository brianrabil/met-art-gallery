Impressionism’s sun-soaked hues are trending again, but this time they’re pairing with smart-lighting presets, sustainable fabrics, and AI-matched wall art. Here are the five palettes shaking up showrooms.

## 1. Mist-Blue Monets

*Hero Painting:* **“La Grenouillère”** (1869)  
**Palette:** Lake-Blue #6A9DE0 · Reed-Green #8AA783 · Cloud-Gray #D4D9DF  
**Why it works:** Cools down south-facing rooms without feeling icy; pairs with off-white boucle sofas.

## 2. Renoir Rosewood

*Hero Painting:* **“Two Young Girls at the Piano”** (1892)  
**Palette:** Rosewood #B37877 · Lace-Cream #F6EFE8 · Walnut-Brown #7D5B4A  
**Why it works:** Adds warmth to minimalist interiors—think rosewood credenzas against micro-cement floors.

## 3. Pissarro Pewter

*Hero Painting:* **“Boulevard Montmartre, Winter Morning”** (1897)  
**Palette:** Pewter-Gray #8F9297 · Fog-White #E9ECF0 · Taxi-Yellow #E8BF60  
**Why it works:** Industrial lofts get an update with a soft, metropolitan mist.

## 4. Degas Ballet Blush

*Hero Painting:* **“The Dancing Class”** (1874)  
**Palette:** Ballet-Blush #EBCCD4 · Tutu-Ivory #F5F1EE · Stage-Shadow #524C46  
**Why it works:** Feminine without being saccharine; pops next to aged brass fixtures.

## 5. Sisley Skyfade

*Hero Painting:* **“The Bridge at Villeneuve-la-Garenne”** (1872)  
**Palette:** Skyfade-Blue #A3C1DE · River-Steel #6A7F99 · Marsh-Olive #8D906E  
**Why it works:** Evokes outdoor serenity—ideal for spa bathrooms and meditation corners.
